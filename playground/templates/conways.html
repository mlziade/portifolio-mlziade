Welcome to Conway's Game of Life!

This is an interactive implementation of John Conway's famous cellular automaton. The Game of Life is a zero-player game, meaning its evolution is determined by its initial state, requiring no further input.

You can:

  • Click on cells to toggle them alive/dead
  • Drag to pan around the infinite grid
  • Use the controls to start, stop, or clear the simulation
  • Adjust the animation speed with the delay slider

Try creating some patterns and watch them evolve!

The Rules

Conway's Game of Life follows four simple rules:

  1. Underpopulation: Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbors dies, as if by underpopulation.
  2. Survival: Any live cell with two or three live neighbors lives on to the next generation.
  3. Overpopulation: Any live cell with more than three live neighbors dies, as if by overpopulation.
  4. Reproduction: Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbors becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.

These simple rules create complex and fascinating patterns that can evolve in unexpected ways!
